  10. On the Day when We will gather them al- together, then say to those who ascribed part- ners, “To your place, you and your partners.” Then We will separate between them, and their partners will say, “It was not us you were worshiping.”

  11. “Allah is sufficient witness between us and you. We were unaware of your worshiping us.”

  12. There, every soul will experience what it had done previously; and they will be returned to Allah, their True Master; and what they used to invent will fail them.

  13. Say, “Who provides for you from the heaven and the earth? And who controls the hearing and the sight? And who produces the living from the dead, and produces the dead from the living? And who governs the Order?” They will say, “Allah.” Say, “Will you not be careful?”

  14. Such is Allah, your Lord—the True. What is there, beyond the truth, except falsehood? How are you turned away?

  15. Thus your Lord’s Word proved true against those who disobeyed, for they do not believe.

  16. Say, “Can any of your partners initiate cre- ation, and then repeat it?” Say, “Allah initi- ates creation, and then repeats it. How are you so deluded?”

  17. Say, “Can any of your partners guide to the truth?” Say, “Allah guides to the truth. Is He who guides to the truth more worthy of being followed, or he who does not guide, unless he himself is guided? What is the matter with you? How do you judge?”

  18. Most of them follow nothing but assump- tions; and assumptions avail nothing against the truth. Allah is fully aware of what they do.

  19. This Quran could not have been produced by anyone other than Allah. In fact, it is a confirmation of what preceded it, and an elaboration of the Book. There is no doubt about it—it is from the Lord of the Universe.

  20. Or do they say, “He has forged it”? Say, “Then produce a single chapter like it, and call upon whomever you can, apart from Al- lah, if you are truthful.”

  21. In fact, they deny what is beyond the limits of their knowledge, and whose explanation has not yet reached them. Thus those before them refused to believe. So note the conse- quences for the wrongdoers.

  22. Among them are those who believe in it, and among them are those who do not be- lieve in it. Your Lord is fully aware of the mis- chief-makers.

  23. If they accuse you of lying, say, “I have my deeds, and you have your deeds. You are quit of what I do, and I am quit of what you do.”

  24. And among them are those who listen to you. But can you make the deaf hear, even though they do not understand?

  25. And among them are those who look at you. But can you guide the blind, even though they do not see?

  26. Allah does not wrong the people in the least, but the people wrong their own selves.

  27. On the Day when He rounds them up—as if they had tarried only one hour of a day— they will recognize one another. Those who denied the meeting with Allah will be the los- ers. They were not guided.

  28. Whether We show you some of what We promise them, or take you, to Us is their re- turn. Allah is witness to everything they do.

  29. Every community has a messenger. When their messenger has come, judgment will be passed between them with fairness, and they will not be wronged.

  30. And they say, “When will this promise be fulfilled, if you are truthful?”

  31. Say, “I have no power to harm or benefit myself, except as Allah wills. To every nation is an appointed time. Then, when their time arrives, they can neither postpone it by one hour, nor advance it.

  32. Say, “Have you considered? If His punishment overtakes you by night or by day, what part of it will the guilty seek to hasten?”

  33. “Then, when it falls, will you believe in it? Now? When before you tried to hasten it?”

  34. Then it will be said to those who did wrong, “Taste the torment of eternity. Will you be re- warded except for what you used to do?”

  35. And they inquire of you, “Is it true?” Say, “Yes, by my Lord, it is true, and you cannot evade it.”

  36. Had every soul which had done wrong pos- sessed everything on earth, it would offer it as a ransom. They will hide the remorse when they witness the suffering, and it will be judged between them equitably, and they will not be wronged.

  37. Assuredly, to Allah belongs everything in the heavens and the earth. Assuredly, the promise of Allah is true. But most of them do not know.

  38. He gives life and causes death, and to Him you will be returned.

  39. O people! There has come to you advice from your Lord, and healing for what is in the hearts, and guidance and mercy for the be- lievers. Say, “In Allah’s grace and mercy let them rejoice. That is better than what they hoard.”

  40. Say, “Have you considered the sustenance Allah has sent down for you, some of which you made unlawful, and some lawful?” Say, “Did Allah give you permission, or do you fabricate lies and attribute them to Allah?”

  41. What will they think—those who fabricate lies and attribute them to Allah—on the Day of Resurrection? Allah is bountiful towards the people, but most of them do not give thanks.

  42. You do not get into any situation, nor do you recite any Quran, nor do you do any- thing, but We are watching over you as you undertake it. Not even the weight of an atom, on earth or in the sky, escapes your Lord, nor is there anything smaller or larger, but is in a clear record.

  43. Unquestionably, Allah’s friends have noth- ing to fear, nor shall they grieve.

  44. Those who believe and are aware. For them is good news in this life, and in the Hereafter. There is no alteration to the words of Allah. That is the supreme triumph.

  45. And let not their sayings dishearten you.

  46. Certainly, to Allah belongs everyone in the heavens and everyone on earth. Those who invoke other than Allah do not follow part- ners; they follow only assumptions, and they only guess.

  47. It is He who made the night for your rest, and the daylight for visibility. Surely in that are signs for people who listen.

  48. And they said, “Allah has taken a son.” Be He glorified. He is the Self-Sufficient. His is everything in the heavens and everything on earth. Do you have any proof for this? Or are you saying about Allah what you do not know?

  49. Say, “Those who fabricate lies about Allah will not succeed.”

  51. Some enjoyment in this world; then to Us is their return; then We will make them taste the severe punishment on account of their disbelief.

  52. And relate to them the story of Noah, when he said to his people, “O my people, if my presence among you and my reminding you of Allah’s signs is too much for you, then in Allah I have put my trust. So come to a deci- sion, you and your partners, and do not let the matter perplex you; then carry out your decision on me, and do not hold back.”

  53. “But if you turn away, I have not asked you for any wage. My wage falls only on Allah, and I was commanded to be of those who submit.”

  54. But they denounced him, so We saved him and those with him in the Ark, and We made them successors, and We drowned those who rejected Our signs. So consider the fate of those who were warned.

  55. Then, after him, We sent messengers to their people. They came to them with the clear proofs, but they would not believe in an- ything they had already rejected. Thus We set a seal on the hearts of the hostile.

  56. Then, after them, We sent Moses and Aa- ron with Our proofs to Pharaoh and his dig- nitaries. But they acted arrogantly. They were sinful people.

  57. And when the truth came to them from Us, they said, “This is clearly sorcery.”

  58. Moses said, “Is this what you say of the truth when it has come to you? Is this sor- cery? Sorcerers do not succeed.”

  59. They said, “Did you come to us to divert us from what we found our ancestors following, and so that you become prominent in the land? We will never believe in you.”

  60. Pharaoh said, “Bring me every experienced sorcerer.”

  61. And when the sorcerers came, Moses said to them, “Throw whatever you have to throw.”

  62. And when they threw, Moses said, “What you produced is sorcery, and Allah will make it fail. Allah does not foster the efforts of the corrupt.”

  63. “And Allah upholds the truth with His words, even though the sinners detest it.”

  64. But none believed in Moses except some children of his people, for fear that Pharaoh and his chiefs would persecute them. Phar- aoh was high and mighty in the land. He was a tyrant.

  65. Moses said, “O my people, if you have be- lieved in Allah, then put your trust in Him, if you have submitted.”

  66. They said, “In Allah we have put our trust. Our Lord, do not make us victims of the op- pressive people.”

  67. “And deliver us, by Your mercy, from the disbelieving people.”

  68. And We inspired Moses and his brother, “Settle your people in Egypt, and make your homes places of worship, and perform the prayer, and give good news to the believers.”

  69. Moses said, “Our Lord, you have given Pharaoh and his chiefs splendor and wealth in the worldly life. Our Lord, for them to lead away from Your path. Our Lord, obliterate their wealth, and harden their hearts, they will not believe until they see the painful tor- ment.”

  70. He said, “Your prayer has been answered, so go straight, and do not follow the path of those who do not know.”

  71. And We delivered the Children of Israel across the sea. Pharaoh and his troops pur- sued them, defiantly and aggressively. Until, when he was about to drown, he said, “I be- lieve that there is no god except the One the Children of Israel believe in, and I am of those who submit.”

  72. Now? When you have rebelled before, and been of the mischief-makers?

  73. Today We will preserve your body, so that you become a sign for those after you. But most people are heedless of Our signs.

  74. And We settled the Children of Israel in a position of honor, and provided them with good things. They did not differ until knowledge came to them. Your Lord will judge between them on the Day of Resurrec- tion regarding their differences.

  75. If you are in doubt about what We revealed to you, ask those who read the Scripture be- fore you. The truth has come to you from your Lord, so do not be of those who doubt.

  76. And do not be of those who deny Allah’s revelations, lest you become one of the losers.

  77. Those against whom your Lord’s Word is justified will not believe.

  78. Even if every sign comes to them—until they see the painful punishment.

  79. If only there was one town that believed and benefited by its belief. Except for the peo- ple of Jonah. When they believed, We re- moved from them the suffering of disgrace in the worldly life, and We gave them comfort for a while.

  80. Had your Lord willed, everyone on earth would have believed. Will you compel people to become believers?

  81. No soul can believe except by Allah’s leave; and He lays disgrace upon those who refuse to understand.

  82. Say, “Look at what is in the heavens and the earth.” But signs and warnings are of no avail for people who do not believe.

  83. Do they expect anything but the likes of the days of those who passed away before them? Say, “Then wait, I will be waiting with you.”

  84. Then We save Our messengers and those who believe. It is binding on Us to save the believers.

  85. Say, “O people, if you are in doubt about my religion—I do not serve those you serve apart from Allah. But I serve Allah, the one who will terminate your lives. And I was commanded to be of the believers.”

  86. And dedicate yourself to the true reli- gion—a monotheist—and never be of the polytheists.

  87. And do not call, apart from Allah, on what neither benefits you nor harms you. If you do, you are then one of the wrongdoers.

  88. If Allah afflicts you with harm, none can remove it except He. And if He wants good for you, none can repel His grace. He makes it reach whomever He wills of His servants. He is the Forgiver, the Merciful.

  89. Say, “O people, the truth has come to you from your Lord. Whoever accepts guidance is guided for his own soul; and whoever strays only strays to its detriment. I am not a guard- ian over you.”

  90. And follow what is revealed to you, and be patient until Allah issues His judgment, for He is the Best of judges.
